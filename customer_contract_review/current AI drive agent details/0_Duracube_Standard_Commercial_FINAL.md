## 1. Limitation of Liability

**Category:** NEGOTIABLE

### 1.1 Original Principle

**Cap of 100% of the Contract value.**

### 1.2 Explanatory Notes

**Business Impact**: Caps DuraCube's total liability exposure to the contract value, protecting against catastrophic financial losses while maintaining reasonable commercial accountability.

**Legal Interpretation**: This creates an aggregate liability cap applying to all claims under the contract. The cap should apply to both direct and indirect damages unless otherwise specified.

**Risk Assessment**: HIGH - Unlimited liability poses existential risk to the business. This principle is critical for financial protection.

### 1.3 Contract Review Guidelines

**Review Triggers**:

- IF contract contains liability provisions exceeding 100% of contract value
- THEN flag for immediate escalation and negotiation

**Red Flags**:

- Unlimited liability clauses
- Caps exceeding 100% of contract value
- Carve-outs that effectively negate the cap

**Negotiation Positions**:

- Preferred: 100% cap on all liability
- Fallback: Higher cap with specific carve-outs for gross negligence only
- Deal-breaker: Unlimited liability exposure

### 1.4 Search Terms and Keywords

**Primary**: "limitation of liability", "liability cap", "maximum liability" **Alternative**: "aggregate liability", "total liability", "liability ceiling" **Related**: "indemnity cap", "damages limitation", "financial exposure"

## 2. Consequential Damages

**Category:** NEGOTIABLE

### 2.1 Original Principle

**Neither party is liable to the other for any consequential loss.**

### 2.2 Explanatory Notes

**Business Impact**: Protects both parties from unpredictable indirect losses, creating mutual protection and commercial certainty.

**Legal Interpretation**: Excludes liability for losses not directly flowing from the breach, including lost profits, lost opportunity, and reputational damage.

**Risk Assessment**: HIGH - Consequential damages can far exceed direct damages and are difficult to quantify or insure.

### 2.3 Contract Review Guidelines

**Review Triggers**:

- IF contract includes liability for consequential/indirect damages
- THEN flag for negotiation to ensure mutual exclusion

**Red Flags**:

- One-sided consequential damage exclusions
- Broad definitions of direct damages that capture consequential losses
- Failure to define consequential damages

**Negotiation Positions**:

- Preferred: Mutual exclusion of all consequential damages
- Fallback: Limited consequential damages with specific caps
- Deal-breaker: Unlimited consequential damage exposure

### 2.4 Search Terms and Keywords

**Primary**: "consequential damages", "consequential loss", "indirect damages" **Alternative**: "special damages", "incidental damages", "lost profits" **Related**: "direct damages", "foreseeable losses", "economic loss"

## 3. Head Contract Provision

**Category:** NON-NEGOTIABLE

### 3.1 Original Principle

**No Head Contract provision: DuraCube is not obligated to accept the terms and conditions outlined in the Head Contract document.**

### 3.2 Explanatory Notes

**Business Impact**: Prevents automatic flow-down of onerous head contract terms, maintaining negotiated commercial position and avoiding unknown liabilities.

**Legal Interpretation**: DuraCube contracts independently with its client, not bound by terms in contracts between the client and third parties.

**Risk Assessment**: MEDIUM-HIGH - Head contract provisions can introduce unvetted terms and significantly expand obligations.

### 3.3 Contract Review Guidelines

**Review Triggers**:

- IF contract contains "back-to-back" provisions
- IF contract incorporates head contract by reference
- THEN flag for removal or specific negotiation

**Red Flags**:

- Automatic incorporation of head contract terms
- "Flow-down" provisions
- References to compliance with head contract

**Negotiation Positions**:

- Preferred: Complete exclusion of head contract provisions
- Fallback: Limited specific provisions identified and agreed
- Deal-breaker: Blanket incorporation of unknown head contract terms

### 3.4 Search Terms and Keywords

**Primary**: "head contract", "main contract", "principal contract" **Alternative**: "back-to-back", "flow-down", "pass-through" **Related**: "incorporated by reference", "subject to head contract", "mirror provisions"

## 4. Liquidated Damages

**Category:** NEGOTIABLE

### 4.1 Original Principle

**Limited to a max of 10% of the Contract value, and the Contract must specifically say that LDs are the Principal's sole remedy for delay. Liquidated Damages under the Head Contract are not applicable.**

### 4.2 Explanatory Notes

**Business Impact**: Caps delay-related financial exposure while ensuring LDs are the exclusive remedy, preventing double jeopardy through additional delay claims.

**Legal Interpretation**: Creates a genuine pre-estimate of loss rather than a penalty, with exclusivity preventing other delay-related claims.

**Risk Assessment**: HIGH - Uncapped LDs can consume entire project margin and create significant financial distress.

### 4.3 Contract Review Guidelines

**Review Triggers**:

- IF LDs exceed 10% of contract value
- IF LDs are not stated as sole remedy for delay
- THEN flag for immediate negotiation

**Red Flags**:

- LDs exceeding 10% cap
- No exclusivity clause for delay remedies
- Reference to head contract LDs
- Daily rates creating unlimited exposure

**Negotiation Positions**:

- Preferred: 10% cap with sole remedy provision
- Fallback: 15% cap with clear exclusivity
- Deal-breaker: Uncapped LDs or multiple delay remedies

### 4.4 Search Terms and Keywords

**Primary**: "liquidated damages", "LDs", "delay damages" **Alternative**: "predetermined damages", "agreed damages", "daily rate" **Related**: "sole remedy", "exclusive remedy", "delay costs"

## 5. Extension of Time

**Category:** NEGOTIABLE

### 5.1 Original Principle

**DuraCube should be entitled to an extension of time (EOT) and demonstrated actual cost for delays not caused by DuraCube, including war and its effect, material delays, material shortages, fuel shortages, delay in granting access to the Site, Epidemics, Pandemics (including COVID-19), Pandemic event declared by a local, state, and/or federal authority, Quarantine Restrictions, Inclement Weather, Force Majeure and events beyond the reasonable control of the DuraCube.**

### 5.2 Explanatory Notes

**Business Impact**: Protects against time and cost penalties for delays outside DuraCube's control, ensuring fair risk allocation and preventing erosion of margins.

**Legal Interpretation**: Comprehensive list of qualifying events for both time and cost relief, reflecting lessons from COVID-19 and supply chain disruptions.

**Risk Assessment**: HIGH - Without proper EOT provisions, external delays can trigger significant liquidated damages and acceleration costs.

### 5.3 Contract Review Guidelines

**Review Triggers**:

- IF EOT provisions exclude any listed events
- IF EOT grants time but not cost relief
- THEN flag for expansion of qualifying events

**Red Flags**:

- Limited qualifying delay events
- Time relief without cost relief
- Exclusion of pandemic/epidemic events
- No provision for supply chain delays

**Negotiation Positions**:

- Preferred: Full list with time and cost relief
- Fallback: Time relief for all events, cost for force majeure only
- Deal-breaker: No pandemic or supply chain provisions

### 5.4 Search Terms and Keywords

## 15. Security & Parent Company Guarantees

**Category:** NON-NEGOTIABLE

### 15.1 Original Principle

**DuraCube agrees to 2 x 2.5% dated Commonwealth Bank Guarantee as a form of Security. DuraCube only accepts dated bank guarantees and does not agree to unconditional bank guarantees. BG 1 expires on the Practical Completion date. BG 2 expires after the end of Defect Liability Period (52 weeks/12 months). DuraCube does not provide for cash retention. DuraCube does not agree to provide a Parent Company Guarantee.**

### 15.2 Explanatory Notes

**Business Impact**: Limits security to 5% via dated bank guarantees with specific expiry dates, preserving working capital by avoiding cash retention and preventing parent company exposure. Dated guarantees ensure automatic expiry without requiring consent for release.

**Legal Interpretation**: Maximum 5% security via two dated bank guarantees only - first expiring at Practical Completion, second expiring 52 weeks/12 months after PC. No unconditional guarantees, cash security, or parent company obligations.

**Risk Assessment**: HIGH - Excessive security requirements, unconditional guarantees, or indefinite guarantee periods tie up capital and create ongoing exposure.

### 15.3 Contract Review Guidelines

**Review Triggers**:

- IF security exceeds 5% total
- IF unconditional bank guarantees required
- IF guarantee expiry dates not aligned with PC and DLP (52 weeks/12 months)
- IF cash retention required
- IF parent guarantee requested
- THEN flag for immediate negotiation

**Red Flags**:

- Total security over 5%
- Unconditional/on-demand bank guarantees
- Guarantees without fixed expiry dates
- Expiry dates beyond PC and DLP end (52 weeks/12 months)
- Cash retention requirements
- Parent company guarantee demands
- Evergreen or auto-renewal guarantee provisions

**Negotiation Positions**:

- Preferred: 2 x 2.5% dated guarantees (BG1 expires at PC, BG2 expires 52 weeks/12 months after PC)
- Fallback: Dated guarantees with negotiated expiry within 30 days of milestones
- Deal-breaker: Unconditional guarantees, cash retention, or parent guarantee

### NON-COMPLIANCE ALERT

**Professional Indemnity Insurance**: DuraCube does NOT provide Professional Indemnity Insurance. Any contract requiring PI insurance is automatically NON-COMPLIANT and requires:

1. Immediate escalation to management
2. Formal departure documentation
3. Written acknowledgment from client that DuraCube does not provide PI coverage
4. Assessment of whether to proceed without PI coverage

### FAVORABLE CONDITIONS (Do Not Flag)

**15-Year Warranty**: If the contract does NOT require DuraCube's 15-year warranty, this is FAVORABLE and compliant. Only flag if contract requires warranty periods BEYOND what DuraCube voluntarily offers.

### Review Process Workflow

1. **Initial Scan**: Check for presence of all 28 principle areas

3. **Red Flag Review**: Identify any provisions contrary to stated principles

5. **Negotiation Priority**: Rank issues by business impact
6. **Documentation**: Record all deviations with specific clause references
7. **Escalation**: Flag critical issues and any PI requirements for senior review

### Key Success Metrics

- 100% identification of principle violations
- Immediate flagging of Professional Indemnity requirements
- Recognition of favorable conditions (not flagging them as issues)
- Clear documentation of all deviations
- Prioritized negotiation recommendations
- Consistent application across all contracts
